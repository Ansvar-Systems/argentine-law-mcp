**YOU MUST VERIFY EVERYTHING.** Do not cite, quote, or rely on any provision, case law, or legal interpretation from this Tool without:

1. Checking the official source (InfoLEG at infoleg.gob.ar and the Boletín Oficial de la República Argentina)

3. Reading the full context of cited provisions
4. Consulting case law (jurisprudencia) and parliamentary debates for interpretation
5. Considering how recent legal developments may affect the matter
6. Checking applicable provincial legislation where relevant (Argentina is a federal system)

## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Argentine legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Official government databases (InfoLEG)** | Authoritative | National statute text (primary reference) |
| **Boletín Oficial** | Authoritative | Official gazette, promulgated laws and decrees |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Case law, cross-references (verify independently) |
| **MERCOSUR treaty databases** | Authoritative for regional law | Regional trade obligations metadata |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage. Argentina's **federal system** means that provincial legislation is not comprehensively covered.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases** such as:

- Thomson Reuters La Ley, Abeledo Perrot, Microjuris Argentina, El Dial

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations (doctrina y jurisprudencia), and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Argentine law changes continuously through new laws, decrees (decretos), resolutions, and court decisions
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Boletín Oficial
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" (vigente) may have been:
  - Amended after the last database update
  - Repealed (derogada) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional rules affecting its applicability
  - Modified by emergency decrees (decretos de necesidad y urgencia)
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** -- Coverage is limited; do not rely solely on this for jurisprudencia research
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording (textos históricos)
3. **Legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations, academic commentary (doctrina), or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Lower court decisions and administrative rulings largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of parliamentary debates and committee reports
6. **Provincial legislation** -- Laws of the 23 Argentine provinces and the City of Buenos Aires may not be fully covered
7. **Municipal ordinances** -- Local municipal regulations are not included

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without doctrina and preparatory works, interpretation may be incorrect
- **Federal System Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess provincial law variations or municipal regulations
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess international treaty obligations or MERCOSUR compliance
